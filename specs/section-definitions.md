# Section Definitions

This file defines the fixed structure of the Lingyun AI Morning Brief.

## Hero Module

Purpose: create a strong first impression and make the email feel like an editorial product, not a raw research dump.

Must include:

- Dynamic daily headline
- Date
- Short editorial deck
- One visual placeholder or relevant image slot
- Clear branding as Lingyun's AI Morning Brief

## Market Note Module

Purpose: provide quick exchange-rate context for Lingyun.

Placement: small card immediately after the Executive Summary.

Must include:

- GBP → AUD exchange rate
- GBP → CNY exchange rate
- As-of time if available
- The phrase "Indicative only"

Do not replace this with a generic market/product note.

## 1. Executive Summary

Purpose: give Lingyun the day in one scan.

Format:

- Gmail version should be 120-180 words
- Include today's strongest AI industry signal
- Explain why it matters for AI Engineer / Applied AI Engineer roles
- Include one practical takeaway for Lingyun
- Focus on decisions, launches, technical shifts, builder trends, and job-market signals
- Avoid generic commentary

## 2. Top AI News

Purpose: capture important AI industry movement.

Each item should include:

- Headline
- 2-3 sentence summary
- Why it matters
- Source link

Recommended count: 3-5 items.

## 3. Builder Spotlight

Purpose: highlight external builders, teams, products, or demos.

Must include:

- Who built it
- What they shipped
- Link to demo, product, post, or repository
- Why it is interesting for AI builders

This section should contain external builders/products, not only advice for Lingyun.

## 4. Cool AI Projects / Fun AI Apps / Daily AI Builder Gallery

Preferred production title: **Daily AI Builder Gallery**.

Purpose: discover what people are actually building with AI.

Must include exactly 5 real, already-published external AI apps, projects, demos, workflows, agents, tools, or experiments.

Each entry must include:

- Project name
- What it does
- Original release/demo link
- Optional secondary discussion link
- Stack/framework if publicly known
- Why people are talking about it

Rules:

- Prioritise projects released or actively discussed in the last 7 days.
- Use original links such as X/Twitter, GitHub, YouTube, Product Hunt, Hacker News, personal blog, or official site.
- Do not include "Inspiration for Lingyun".
- Do not include "Portfolio lesson".
- Do not force relevance to Lingyun's projects.

## 5. Open Source Radar

Purpose: track practical open-source AI engineering tools and releases.

Each item should include:

- Repo/project name
- What changed or why it is notable
- GitHub/source link
- Practical use case

Recommended count: 3-5 items.

## 6. AI Engineering Insight

Purpose: provide one useful technical insight Lingyun can apply.

Possible topics:

- RAG design
- Agent architecture
- Eval patterns
- Observability
- Prompt/system design
- Data pipelines
- Model routing
- Cost, latency, or reliability trade-offs

The section should be concrete and implementation-minded.

## 7. AI Job Market Signals

Purpose: help Lingyun understand where AI Engineer demand is moving.

Signals may include:

- Hiring trends
- Company role requirements
- Funding announcements
- New AI teams or product groups
- Skill keywords appearing in job posts

Must include at least one job-market, company, hiring, funding, or labour-market source.

## 8. Opportunities for Lingyun

Purpose: translate the day into selective practical opportunities.

Must include exactly 3 visual cards:

1. Feature to build
2. Improvement to an existing project
3. Portfolio/GitHub/LinkedIn idea

Each card must include:

- Signal observed today
- Opportunity
- Suggested action
- Why it helps the job transition

Do not force every news item into a personal action.

## 9. Content Ideas

Purpose: turn research into public learning and visibility.

Must include separate cards for:

- LinkedIn ideas
- X ideas

LinkedIn ideas should be thoughtful and career-positioning. X ideas should be concise and easier to post quickly.

## 10. Today's 1-Hour Action

Purpose: make the brief operational.

Must include:

- Objective
- Why it matters
- 60-minute breakdown
- Output to produce
- Suggested follow-up

The action should be realistic, specific, and finishable in one hour.
